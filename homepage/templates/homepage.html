TESDA Courses Offered

Computer Systems Servicing NCII
08/06/2019

TESDA CSS NC2, or the Technical Education and Skills Development Authority Computer Systems Servicing National Certificate II, focuses on imparting skills in computer hardware, software, and network maintenance. It equips students with the knowledge and expertise to assemble, troubleshoot, and maintain computer systems effectively. The program emphasizes essential IT competencies, making graduates adept at handling computer-related issues and providing technical support. This training opens doors to careers as computer technicians and IT professionals in various industries.

Bread & Pastry NCII
09/27/2022

TESDA Bread and Pastry Production NC II equips students with the skills needed to excel in the baking and pastry industry. This program focuses on developing competencies in preparing and producing various bakery and pastry products, cakes, and desserts. Students learn about ingredient selection, baking techniques, and presentation, enabling them to work in bakeries, pastry shops, or even start their own baking businesses. It's a hands-on training program that provides a solid foundation in the art and science of baking and pastry production.

Dressmaking NCII
09/27/2022

TESDA Dressmaking NC II imparts essential skills for creating ladies' casual apparel in the garment sector. Students learn how to draft and cut patterns, lay them out on fabric, sew the materials together, and add the finishing touches. This program focuses on precise garment construction, fitting, and quality assurance. Graduates are well-prepared to work in the fashion industry, whether in clothing manufacturing, tailoring, or even launching their fashion design businesses, due to the comprehensive knowledge and hands-on experience gained in dressmaking.

WATCH: Growing Food, Saving Lives by Kalimudan Sa Ranao Foundation Incorporated
Basak Malutlut residents are part of the first batch of returnees six months after the May 2017 siege. Some families have been separated during the chaos of the war. They were traumatized by the war, and again from months of living in evacuation centers. They came home to a ghost town. Their houses were ransacked. They had no food and no source of income. The general environment simply was not conducive to recovery and rehabilitation, which Maranaos needed the most. “It was every person for himself/herself for Marawi residents when the war broke out. They stopped caring about their friends, even other members of their family,” says Kimen of Kalimudan. Kalimudan sa Ranao, together with the community of Basak Malutlut in Marawi City, Lanao del Sur, developed an innovative system they call Growing Food, Saving Lives. It is a two-pronged approach which uses family conversations and urban container gardening to bring back the trust among the community and to provide livelihood to residents whose lives were disrupted by the war. This is one of the innovative social systems supported by TUKLAS Innovation Labs.



WATCH: How Is Marawi?
Step into the heart of Marawi as we mark the 4th anniversary of the siege through a compelling video that unfolds the narrative of Marawi Solidarity and Memorialization. Experience the streets filled with people, united in peaceful protest and rallying to emphasize the collective memory of the harrowing events. This poignant video not only captures the solemnity of the occasion but also sheds light on the stories of resilience that have emerged from the aftermath. Through powerful visuals and testimonials, the video highlights the strength of a community that refuses to be defined by tragedy, showcasing the unwavering spirit of those who have come together to rebuild and support one another. As victims' pleas echo through the footage, the video serves as a call to action, urging viewers to empathize with the ongoing struggles faced by those affected. It becomes a compelling testament to the importance of remembrance, solidarity, and the pursuit of justice in the face of adversity. Intricately woven with emotional threads, this video not only honors the past but also stands as a symbol of hope for a brighter and more united future. Join us in commemorating Marawi Solidarity and Memorialization, as we strive to keep the memories alive and work towards a world where such tragedies are never repeated.
