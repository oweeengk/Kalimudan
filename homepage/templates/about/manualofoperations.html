PERSONNEL POLICIES


DEFINITION

Personnel refer to all persons not a member. Employee of officially connected to KFI whose service are directly paid for implementation of the organization policies and under specific terms in an agreement contract duly concurred by the organization through its duly constituted official representatives.

BASIC PRINCIPLES

KFI commits the directly and full use of its human resources for the efficient implementation of its mission, vision and goal while affording it a system of personnel policies and procedure that upholds the dignity of labor, promotes full employment insures equal work opportunities regardless of sex, race or creed and fosters team spirit and collective work.

HIRING AND SELECTION

1. Policy statement

KRFI shall recruit highly competent and committed individuals to fill the existing vacant position in the organizational structure/plantilla for its current project term. Applicants shall be given competitive examination and interviews and shall be selected on the basis of their qualification and fitness to perform the duties and assure responsibilities congruent to their position. Hiring of personnel for the KRFI operation shall be sole responsibility of the KRFI bodies.

2. Implementing guidelines

a) Sourcing of applicants
Recruitment of new members shall be the concern of the whole organization. Any personnel officer can recommend persons qualified to fill the vacancies. Sourcing may come from within or outside the institution, however qualified applicants from within shall give priority over those from outside (promotion or transfer of competent employees) if all relevant factors such as general qualifications and experience or relatively equal. All applicants shall be evaluated on the basis of their willingness to actively participate intake realization of organization mission, vision and goals; their competence as measured by results of competitive exams and interviews administered by Kalimudan; and their minimum experience and education requirement of the post applied for.

Procedural guidelines
The following procedure are deemed necessary:

a) Applicants are required to accomplish in full KFI's Personnel information sheet and submit their curriculum vitae. Official transcriopt of records and samples of written work deemed necessary.
b)The KRFI management shall initially screen applicant. Based on submitted documents the BOT shall evaluate the credentials of the applicant in relation the position he/she is applying for.
c) Applicants passing the initial screening are forwarded by the Executive Director to the screening committee.
d) The screening committee shall conduct an interview with the applicants in the presence of Executive Director to determine the qualification and eligibility of the applicant.
e) Skills tests shall be given to applicants whose credential are deemed appropriate the position applied for.
f) Based on submitted documents and background checks, the management will further consult with screening committee before finally forwarding recommendation to the chairman of the Board.

Pre-employment requirements

The newly hired employee shall submit to Kalimudan the following:
   a) Certificate of training attended, when applicable
   b) Clearance of liability from former employer
   c) Medical Certificate
   d) SSS and TIN, when applicable

Procedural guidelines for the transfer/reclassification/rehiring of personnel


Personnel movement such as transfer, reclassification, and Rehiring shall be subject to the same guidelines and procedures as for newly hired employees (refer to guidelines on recruitment / Hiring)

Employment Status

Policy Statement

Being a non-stock or non-profit organization is highly dependent on grants to fund its program implementation and operation. KFI is bound to hire project employees whose appointments are coterminous with the project term for and within which he/she is hired or with the availability of funds.

Implementing Guidelines

To determine differences in remuneration however, employment status of KFI Personnel shall be classified as follows.

Regular

A regular personnel is one who holds a regular position in take plantilla of the KFI for its defined project term and has satisfactory completed his/her probationary period of employment.

Probationary

Newly hired personnel undergone a probationary period for a minimum of three months or a maximum of six months at the fifth month of which he/she is evaluated before being appointed to regular status. A less than FLAT AVERAGE rating 3.0 (using the KFI evaluation scheme) in the evaluation will mean automatic termination for the probationary personnel.

Contractual

Contractual personnel is one who is for a specific project task, program or special duty the termination of which has been predetermined at the time of his/her engagement.

Consultant is also contractual personnel. He/she is hired for a fixed term provides specialized technical assistance in his/her field of expertise.

Employment Compensation and Benefits

Policy statement

KFI shall administer compensation and benefits in a manner that rewards the relative importance of the personnel corresponding to his/her and position in the organization. It shall also consider the individual social needs and welfare based on generally accepted standards and funding capacity of the partnerships.

Implementation Guidelines

Salary determination

All KFI personnel shall receive salaries consistent with KFI; rank and salary scale.

Salaries of personnel shall be determined on the basis of qualification, experience, skills and level of responsibility. Periodic review of the effective salary scale shall be done to make it at par with prevailing rates of to her NGOs and academic community.

Benefits

a) SSS, PhilHealth, and Pag-ibig Benefits

All employees shall be fully covered by Social Security System (SSS). Phil Health and Pag Ibig and employees’ contribution and compensation benefits covering sick leaves salary loans pag-ibig loans for surviving-g beneficiaries hospitalization benefits and compensation for work--- related disabilities. The organization shall orient and assist its employee’s avail of these benefits to the full extent allowed by the system.

b) 13th month pay

All personnel irrespective of employment status are entitled to a 13th month pay equivalent to one month basic pay.

Personnel who rendered less than a year of service will receive a 13 month pay on a prorate basis computed using the formula:

Effective monthly rate x no. of months of service divide by 12 months.

c) Tenure pay

Personnel who have rendered continuous service for a minimum of two years are entitled to a tenure pay.

This amounts to 200.00 per year of service and is receivable every end of the month starting on his/her 2nd anniversary as an employee of the organization.

d) Loan benefits

Subject to the availability of funds, regular employees can avail of a salary loan amounting to the equivalent of their one month salary. The loan is payable within twelve months through automatic salary deduction every fifteen days with a two percent interest based on remaining balance. The first payment is done on the first pay day following the acquisition of the loan. Any previous loan must be cleared full settled first before succeeding request is entertained. Resigning staff member must be cleared of all loans.

e) Leave benefits

All regular personnel who have rendered one continuous year of service (probationary or contractual period included) are entitled to the following leave of absence with pay.

   a. 15 working days vacations leave
   b. 15 working days sick leave
   c. 30 days calendar maternity leave
   d. 10 calendar days for paternity leave

Guidelines for Availment of leave credits

All personnel are required to file their request for leave at least a week before the actual date except in circumstances that do not allow the prior filing of such (e.g. sudden death in the family emergency cases). In case of the latter, the personnel should ensure that the section head and the ED are notified on the day of his/her absence or start of leave. The filling of the application should then be done the day he/she reports for work.

Leave benefits should be earned and not enjoyed in advance. All requests for leave must be settled within the applicants unit and approved by the section head/program coordinator, As mentioned earlier, any unauthorized absences shall be considered absence without official leave AWOL and may be subjected to appropriate sanctions.

All authorized absence beyond the leave credits shall be considered leave without pay.

Accumulation of sick leave benefits is allowable.

Request of regular personnel who wish to go on leave for personal reasons for a period beyond the leave credits available to him/her must be approved by the section head/program co-ordinator and Executive director.

Other welfare benefits

Once a personnel attains regular status, he/she is entitled to staff welfare benefits, these benefits, however are contingent on the existence of the staff welfare fund.

Medicare/Medicine Allowance

Health insurance program shall be employed to cover regular medical care hospitalization and other related services.

Personnel Action

It is Kalimudan ED who approves any action related to personnel. Recommendation for personnel action may be done by section head/program co-ordinator and then coursed through the ED or by the personnel upon prior consultation with the concerned section management (Head/Program Co-ordinator).

The following are the definitions of personnel action.

Regular, Probationary, contractual appointment

Promotion

Advancement in rank with corresponding increases in pay and in duties and responsibilities. Personnel lined-up for promotion shall undergo a three-month development assignment during which there will be no charge in the concerned personnel rank and salary after which he/she will be subject to evaluation. Upon confirmation of promotion, any increases in salary will be retroactive to the start of his/her development assignment.

Reclassification

Change in position with corresponding change on duties/responsibilities, provided that there will be no diminution of benefits for the concerned personnel and provided further that reclassification resulting to demotion in rank personnel being reclassified.

Transfer

Movement from one group / department or branch to another without changing one’s position, rank and salary. Transfer is permanent and the staff member’s connection with the former, group/department / branch shall be severed. Transferee’s salary shall be charged to the new unit.

Resignation

Voluntary action by a staff member to terminate his/her services from the organization submission of a resignation letter must be done at least a month prior to its date of affectivity.

Resigning employees must comply with the necessary clearances and requirements prior to their disengagement form the organization

Termination

Conclusion of employment upon the recommendation of the section management even prior to termination date of appointment/contract it may be due to poor personnel evaluation results or to the position being closed for redundancy/cessation of operation of a unit.

Termination of appointment also applies to regular appointees for reason such an administrative sanction against the personnel (dishonorable dismissal).

Employees subjected to termination must comply with the necessary clearances and requirements prior to their disengagement from the organization.

Staff Evaluation

Policy statement

The KFI shall conduct regular staff evaluation aimed at upgrading human resources capability. Evaluation will help every personnel to gauge his/her current performance as well a potential for further growth and development. Its benefits will redound to professional growth as well as to better services by the personnel to the KFI.

Evaluation Instrument

All personnel shall undergo evaluation without exception

Schedule of staff evaluation shall be as follows:

Newly hired – fifth month employment or one month before the end of the probationary period and sixth month of regular status.

Regular personnel

Regular personnel is subject to annual evaluation (Using the KFI’s personnel Evaluation Scheme) as contained in his/her appointment paper. A less than FLAT AVERAGE rating of 3.0 during the last evaluation period automatically puts the personnel into serious review for three months at the end of which he/she will again be evaluated another less than flat average rating 3.0 this time will become grounds for termination.

Personnel evaluation shall be conducted in the context of regular unit/institutional assessment and summing-up to provide a framework from which to vie personnel performance.

The staff and management shall arrive at a consensus and unity in viewing the individual personnel performance during the review period.

Evaluation of the Management Committee

The individual personnel shall evaluate on the basis of its capability as an administrative body. Individual members of the committee as well as by the staff of the department he/she directly handles.

Evaluation of staff members

The individual personnel under evaluation shall evaluate him/her. Likewise he/she shall be evaluated by his/her peers, immediate supervisor and or department head.

Personnel evaluation shall be assisted / overseen by the organization ED.

Performance evaluation results shall be collected. Computed and recorded by the KFI ED.

Computed rating shall be the primary basis for personnel action.

Staff communication/Grievances Mechanism

Statement of policy

The KFI shall keep open at all time all channels of communication between the management and staff members. The channels shall be maintained to keep working relation harmonious and to make the entire KFI and efficient, efficient and productive working team.

Implementing Guidelines

Personnel shall at all time, be informed of institutional, work development through staff meetings, bulletins, memos and publications.

Venues for regular dialogues and consultation among the KFI staff shall be provided to thresh out and resolve the matter and issues of mutual interest. The immediate supervisor shall be responsible for setting or arbitrating all personnel problems in his/her area.

The KFI management in coordination with the section head/program, shall assist arbitration settlement, arbitration and settlement of the problems, and in proposing viable solutions.

Procedural Guidelines (Addressing Grievances)

As a first step, The immediate supervisor must confer with the aggrieved party for clarification and objective presentation of issues involved. This is also to place the disagreement in its proper perspective. The section was the disagreement cropped up shall resort to collective resolution.

If the second step does not suffice for a satisfactory settlement the matter will be elevated to next higher body. The president of the executive committee may be requested to participate until the matter is resolved.

If the staff problem involves his immediate superior, he/she may seek the assistance of the KFI management and take the matter up directly to the next higher body.

If the mediation of the KFI Management and the executive committee still does not suffice for satisfactory settlement an appeal can be addressed up to the Regional Governing Board for a final decision.

Personnel Discipline and Sanction

Policy statement

The system of sanction is designed as a support mechanism to ensure that all personnel of the KFI observe and discharge their duties and responsibilities to the best interest of the KFI. This system of sanction is intended to provide management with the necessary measure to enforce decorum at all times and to protect and uphold the right of every employee due to process.

Implementing Guideline

The immediate superior is responsible for instilling positive discipline and handling cases of light offenses in his/her unit. All staff members should be given an orientation on correct behavior expected of him/her in the office. It should not however, be used as a substitute for effective supervision.

When an employee has committed or is reported to have committed any of the offenses listed in the document. The following guideline must be observed before sanction such as suspension or dismissal can be meted out to the concerned personnel.

    a. Two weeks after the executive committee is informed of the commission of the offence the COO must give the concerned personnel a written notice of the charges against him/her silence on the part of the executive committee after two weeks will mean automatic dismissal of the charges against the concerned personnel.

   b. One week after the concerned personnel has received the written notice of the charges against him/her. He/she must send a written reply to the executive committee the freedom to decide in matter based on existing records and execute when appropriate sanction against the concerned personnel.

   c. The executive committee must conduct a hearing within two weeks after their receipt of the written reply of the concerned personnel. During this meeting all parties concerned or involved in the case must be present and the concerned must have a chance to air his/her case. In cases where the written reply on the concerned may constitute sufficient bases for a fair and objective decision. The executive committee may proceed with formulating its decision even without s formal hearing.

   d. The executive committee will pass a written notice of its decision to the concerned section and personnel within a week after the hearing if no further investigation of the case is necessary. In cases where further investigation needs to be carried out a decision must be arrived at within one month after the hearing.

   e. An appeal for the meditation of the BOT can be made.

   f. Decision of the BOT is executory upon promulgation.

In every case involving disciplinary action the dignity of the personnel must be upheld and fair humane and just treatment must be accorded to him/her. In administering disciplinary action. The following guidelines must be observed:

   a. Sensitivity: Cases of disciplinary action shall be treated with utmost propriety at all times. When deemed necessary, cases may be treated with utmost confidentially.

   b. Promptness: Disciplinary action involving suspension or discharge from duty should be done within the soonest time possible.

   c. Impartiality: There should be no double standard of applied in addressing complains or cases of the disagreeing parties.

   d. Consistency: There should be double standard of sanctions for similar offenses by different offenders

   e. Fairness: Erring staff members should be meted with the proper disciplinary action due to him/her.

   f. Non-punitive nature: The basic of any disciplinary action is to prevent future problems. Focus of disciplinay action should be on preventing recurrence of past problems as well as preventing new ones from arising rather than on administering punishment.

   g. Follow – through: Immediate supervisors should plan for immediate follow-up action especially for minor problems. This is to promptly remedy lapses so that they do not persist long to merit more severe disciplinary action.

   i. Damage/mishandling office properties.

   j. Wasting/spoiling materials and resources.

   k. Unauthorized use of office facilities for personal or outsider’s use.

Grave offenses

The following are acts which are considered grave offenses because of the serious negative impact they may cause on the status of the work of KFI in general. These acts are punishable by suspension of minimum of one month to a maximum of two months or outright dismissal:

   a. Insubordination or willful disobedience of the lawful instruction of a superior or he/she representative in connection with official work/business.

   b. Gross and habitual neglect of duties/responsibilities (includes habitual tardiness/under timing and absences.

   c. Fraud or willful breach of trust reposed on the personnel by the management or it’s duly authorized representatives (includes willful violation of safety regulations, theft or office property, divulging confidential information, issuing malicious statement against the institution, participation in smear campaign and tempering of attendance records).

   d. Committing criminal acts punishable by law.

   e. Committing lascivious acts within the office premises.

   f. Malversation of funds of funds.

   g. Sexual opportunism (e.g. maintaining multiple sexual relations; engaging in sexual infidelity or fleshly satisfaction).

   h. Serious misconduct and acts grossly damaging to the management (this includes alcoholism, drug addiction, habitual gambling, engaging in fraudulent transaction for personal gain like offering/receiving grease money, lending money at usurious rates.

   i. Total disregard of the KFI Constitution and by-laws.

Office administration

Attendance monitoring/working hours

Policy statement

All KFI personnel are required to render minimum of eighth of works per day and forty four hours of work per week.

Implementing guidelines

Coverage

All personal regardless of employment status shall be covered by be policy in attendance monitoring and working hours. Personnel holding managerial and supervisory are not covered by the provision on undertake and overtime.

Official working days

The official working days are from Monday to Friday.

Official working hours

Flextime is allowable between 8:00 am and 7:00 pm

Personnel in the administration department and other service functions must be in their designated work stations during official working days and follow the promptly attend to request inquiries and visitor that normally come within business hours. Any variation from the prescribed working hours must be approved by the section head/program coordinator.

All other personnel are given more flexibility consideration of the offering natures of function faceted work of KFI. However the output orientation of the KFI management mandates it to require optimum performance and maximum output from its personnel and pushes it to devise effective monitoring schemes and control mechanism.

The daily time record (DTR) and attendance logbook shall be the official attendance monitoring instruments for recording daily attendance, whereabouts and travels therefore, tampering of entries and taking the logbook from its proper place are prohibited.

Properly accomplished and duly noted DTR’s submitted to the program coordinator within the third week of the month is a requirement for the release of regular salary of KFI staff.

Personnel monitoring forms should be accomplished in triplicate by personnel who are going to undertake official activities outside office premises. The forms should be properly noted by the program/head section. A copy of these forms should be submitted to program/head and administrative personnel before conducting official business activity elsewhere.

Failure to use any of the above mentioned (DTR, logbook, extra business monitoring form, LOA form). To justify absence from the work station of office premises during official hours may become grounds for sanction against the concerned personnel as any without official leave (AWOL).

Break time

The office shall observe two breaking periods during the day:

Lunch break 12:00 to 1:00 pm

Afternoon Coffee break not more the 20 minutes

The one hour lunch period is not part of the regular working hours while the 20 minutes coffee break is.

Under time/absences

Under time shall refer to a deficiency in the number of working hours/days rendered in a day/week.

For regular employees, under time and absences shall be deducted from their vacation leave credits. It all the vacation leave credits have already been consumed, remaining under time and absences will be conducted from the concerned personnel salary.

For probationary personnel under time and absence shall automatically be deducted from the concerned personnel salary.

Consistent undertiming and absenteeism resulting in deficiencies delays in work output however shall be dealt with accordingly and is subject to disciplinary action ranging from warning to dismissal.

Overtime

Overtime shall refer work hours rendered beyond the prescribed number of hours in completing additional workload as assigned and approved by the department head or his/her designated representative.
