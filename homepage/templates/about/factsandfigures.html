Facts and Figures

Vision: Kalimudan envisions an empowered community living in peace with sustainable livelihood and a healthy environment.
Mission: Kalimudan commits itself to enhance the capacities of its partners in development as they strive to build peace while securing their livelihood, improving health standards and nurturing the environment to uplive the well-being of the people of Lanao.
Goals: Kalimudan will be known as a center for dialogue and Maranao organizing. Be a center bridge between the academe and the communities as a source of technology, have developed and discussed a maranao consciousness and control of its own organizational development and management system.

What is Kalimudan?

Kalimudan, a Non-Government Organization (NGO) situated in Marawi City, Lanao Del Sur, has thrived over the years, amassing almost 35 years of experience in its journey. It has evolved into a well-established organization known for its lean and efficient staffing structure. Additionally, Kalimudan benefits from the support of external experts, including consultants and members of the Board who hail from academic backgrounds. In the course of its existence, Kalimudan has expanded its reach to cover 220 barangays in Lanao del Sur and Lanao del Norte, with a staff of 29 dedicated individuals. Its focus extends across various domains, encompassing sustainable resource management, health and literacy initiatives, peacebuilding, and micro-finance projects.


Kalimudan History

In 1988, a group of young professional volunteers, including individuals from the academe and religious organizations, united their efforts to establish Kalimudan. Their shared goal was to voluntarily leverage their skills, talents, and expertise to uplift the impoverished and marginalized segments of society. Over the years, this grassroots initiative evolved into a well-structured and institutionalized Non-Government Organization (NGO). Kalimudan firmly believes in the importance of political stability, intercultural harmony, quality education, and a healthy population as crucial elements for peace and development. This belief fuels their consistent efforts to foster solidarity and harmonious relations among citizens, Civil Society Organizations, and Local Government, all working collaboratively for the betterment of their communities.

Kalimudan's current initiatives span across four key clusters of activities, including major programs, special projects, contracted programs, and relief efforts for internally displaced people. Major programs encompass a wide range of activities such as peacebuilding, sustainable resource management, education, health, and the enhancement of local governance.

Founded in 1988 under the name 'Kalimudan Sa Ranao,' Kalimudan Foundation began as a hub for discussions on how to impart knowledge and skills to local communities. Its transformation into an active community organization took shape when it responded to the aftermath of a prolonged drought and subsequent flooding in 1991-1992, which severely affected nine municipalities in Lanao del Sur. This experience motivated Kalimudan to conduct a series of ecological workshops advocating for the protection of the Lake Lanao environment. Additionally, the organization rallied against illegal logging operations in Lanao, firmly establishing itself as both a social and environmental advocate.

In the years that followed, Kalimudan expanded its efforts in the realms of peace and development. The organization played a pivotal role in the creation of TRIPEACEDEV and became a driving force behind the Ranao Community Development Network (RCDN), a coalition of NGOs hailing from Lanao del Norte and Lanao del Sur.

Since its inception in 1988, Kalimudan has fortified its position as an NGO dedicated to the betterment of the environment, livelihoods, health, and peace within the communities it serves.


Development Philosophies and Management Practices


Kalimudan envisions an empowered community with sustainable livelihood, healthy environment, living in peace and development.

In its mission, Kalimudan aspires to be a centre to raise awareness of partners in development, mobilize, and enable them to take actions on issues and concerns that affect the well-being of the people of Lanao.

Peacebuilding is anchored to all the programs of Kalimudan. It is similar with the gender in which we are serving both men and women sectors in the communities. This will be a part in exercising rights to access for basic services which is a fundamental human right.


Financial System and Risk Management

Kalimudan, being a development NGO in the past almost three decades, has developed its own manual of operation and financial system, policies, and procedures. The staffs and workers of Kalimudan are required to attend review the manual of operation every year. The financial procedures and policies were prepared by an independent auditing. Financial audit is being conducted on yearly basis.

The management team is composed of senior middle line managers who have worked with the institution for more than a decade. High levels of professionalism and dedication, is therefore expected.

The board of trustees (BOT) meets every quarters and every three years, wherein strategic planning review is being conducted to ensure that the appropriate strategies are reviewed to attain the needs of the time.

An average of 7 to 8 millions worth of projects in a year is being implemented by Kalimudan sa Ranao Foundation Incorporated (KRFI).


Accounting Method

To ensure observance of limitations and restrictions placed on the use of resources available to Kalimudan, the foundation accounts are maintained in accordance with the principles of fund accounting. This is the procedure by which resources for various purposes are classified for accounting and reporting purposes into funds established according to their nature and purposes separate accounts are maintained for each fund; however, funds that have similar characteristics are combined into funds groups.

Kalimudan follows the cash basis of accounting for its transactions except for the recognition of depreciation on property and equipments, provision for doubtful accounts and gains or losses arising from restatement of foreign currency cash balances; Consequently, certain revenues and the related assets are recognized when received rather than when earned; and certain expenses are recognized when paid rather than when the obligation is incurred.


Basic Features

The general accounting system of Kalimudan classifies and presents the flow of accounting information from the original source documents to the books of original entry (Journals) and eventually to the book final entry (general ledger); The general ledger is the basis for the preparation of periodic trial balance and various financial reports.
